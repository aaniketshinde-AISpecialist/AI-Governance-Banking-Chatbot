This assessment evaluated the governance posture of an AI-powered customer support chatbot deployed by a retail bank, leveraging a third-party Large Language Model to handle customer queries across account services, transactions, loan management, card operations, and investment products.

The system was assessed across key components including the LLM layer, internal banking APIs, conversation log store, and human escalation workflows.

Seven major risks were identified across the operating environment, with third-party PII leakage, AI hallucination in financial responses, and unauthorized execution of irreversible actions classified as high-impact governance concerns.

Governance controls were designed across preventive, detective, and corrective categories, including:

PII redaction controls
API-grounded response architecture
automated log retention enforcement
mandatory human escalation mechanisms

The governance framework was aligned to the NIST AI Risk Management Framework across the GOVERN, MANAGE, and MEASURE functions, while also considering India-specific regulatory expectations under the DPDP Act and banking-sector operational risk obligations.

Residual risk across assessed control domains was reduced to Medium-to-Low levels through layered governance controls, though continuous monitoring, periodic vendor reassessment, and ongoing human oversight remain essential to maintaining governance effectiveness.

The assessment concludes that AI-enabled customer support can operate within an acceptable risk posture when supported by:

strong governance accountability,
operational controls,
continuous monitoring,
and clearly defined human oversight boundaries.
